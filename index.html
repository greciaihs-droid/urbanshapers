What is Urban Shapers

Urban Shapers is a platform exploring the ways cities shape us — and how we, in turn, shape our cities.

Through storytelling, conversations, research, and community-building, Urban Shapers examines mobility, culture, climate, public space, communication, and everyday urban life from a human-centered perspective.

Cities are not just places we move through. They influence how we connect, what we value, who we meet, and what kind of future we imagine. Urban Shapers exists to make those relationships visible.

Newsletter

The City Between Us

Stories, reflections, and conversations about mobility, culture, climate, and the ways cities shape our everyday lives.

The City Between Us is the newsletter of Urban Shapers. Part essay, part field notes, part ongoing conversation, it explores the spaces between policy and people, infrastructure and experience, movement and belonging.

Some editions may begin with a bike ride. Others with a conversation overheard on a bus, a climate story, or an observation from another city. It's for anyone curious about how cities influence the way we live, connect, move, and imagine our futures.

Because cities are not just built with concrete and asphalt. They are built with stories, relationships, language, and collective imagination. And there is always a city between us.

About

Hi, I'm Grecia Hernández.

I'm passionate about cities, bicycles, and the stories that emerge from public space.

My journey began in 2014 with Birula Radio, a radio project about cycling and urban life that became the gateway to my work in communication, mobility advocacy, and city-making.

Since then, I've collaborated on projects related to sustainable mobility, road safety, urban culture, climate action, and public communication. Today, I serve as Bicycle Mayor of Guadalajara and continue building spaces for learning, dialogue, and action through Urban Shapers.

Urban Shapers brings together my work as a communicator, educator, podcaster, and urban advocate. It's a place to ask questions, share stories, and imagine better cities together.

Why this matters

Cities are one of the most powerful forces shaping our daily lives.

They influence our health, relationships, opportunities, safety, and sense of belonging. Yet many of the conversations about cities remain confined to technical language, policy documents, or professional circles.

Urban Shapers exists to bridge that gap. By translating complex urban issues into accessible stories and conversations, the project helps people understand how mobility, climate, communication, culture, and public space affect everyday life — and how ordinary people can help shape change.

Because creating better cities is not only the responsibility of planners, politicians, or engineers. It belongs to all of us.
